## 3. TOP 3 CONCERNS

**1. On-Time Performance Deterioration (78.1%, -3.1pp YoY)**
The 6.9-point gap to target combined with accelerating year-over-year decline indicates systemic operational issues affecting schedule integrity. This performance level creates cascading delays, elevated costs from irregular operations, crew timeout situations, and direct customer dissatisfaction. Poor OTP is likely the primary driver behind declining NPS and CSAT scores, as reliability is consistently the top factor in airline customer satisfaction. The gap suggests issues spanning ground operations, maintenance turn times, air traffic management coordination, or crew scheduling efficiency.

**2. Customer Loyalty Erosion (NPS: 38, -6 points YoY)**
Net Promoter Score has fallen 14% year-over-year and sits 24% below target, signaling customers are increasingly unwilling to recommend the airline despite filling seats. This disconnect between load factor success and advocacy suggests the airline is benefiting from route strength or pricing rather than service quality, creating vulnerability to competitive pressure. Declining NPS predicts future revenue challenges as customers switch to competitors when alternatives exist, and negative word-of-mouth amplifies through social channels.

**3. Unit Cost Inflation Outpacing Revenue Growth (CASM: +5.6% vs RASM: +7.0%)**
While revenue growth currently exceeds cost growth, the 4.1% budget overrun and accelerating trajectory threaten margin sustainability. The combination of elevated CASM with poor operational performance suggests costs are being driven by inefficiency rather than strategic investment—irregular operations, crew overtime, passenger compensation, and maintenance disruptions all inflate unit costs. Without intervention, the narrowing gap between revenue and cost growth will eliminate profitability advantages.

## 4. PRIORITY RECOMMENDATIONS

### IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (0-30 days)

- **Operations Recovery Task Force**: Establish daily command center focused on OTP improvement through turn-time analysis, ground handling bottleneck identification, and coordination between maintenance, crew scheduling, and station operations to stop performance bleeding.

- **Customer Recovery Protocol**: Implement enhanced service recovery procedures for disrupted passengers, including proactive communication, expedited rebooking, and empowered frontline staff to address complaints immediately and prevent NPS further erosion.

- **Cost Containment Review**: Launch rapid assessment of unplanned expenditures, focusing on irregular operations costs, overtime, and outsourced services to identify immediate waste elimination opportunities.

### SHORT-TERM ACTIONS (30-90 days)

- **Schedule Optimization**: Adjust block times and aircraft rotations to build realistic buffers based on actual performance data, reducing cascading delays even if it requires modest capacity adjustments in congested markets.

- **Ground Operations Improvement**: Deploy lean process improvements at top delay-generating stations, targeting aircraft turns, baggage handling coordination, and fueling/catering efficiency through cross-functional station teams.

- **Revenue Management Enhancement**: Refine pricing algorithms and inventory controls to close the RASM gap through improved segmentation, competitive response protocols, and ancillary revenue optimization.

- **Crew Resource Planning**: Assess crew staffing models and reserve levels to reduce cancellations and overtime costs while improving schedule resilience during disruptions.

### STRATEGIC ACTIONS (90+ days)

- **Operational Excellence Program**: Implement comprehensive performance management system with predictive analytics for delay prevention, maintenance planning optimization, and integrated operations control center capabilities to sustainably achieve 85%+ OTP.

- **Customer Experience Transformation**: Redesign end-to-end journey based on voice-of-customer insights, addressing pain points in booking, airport processes, onboard service, and disruption management to rebuild NPS toward target.

- **Fleet and Network Efficiency Initiative**: Evaluate aircraft utilization, network connectivity, and route profitability to optimize capacity deployment and reduce structural cost disadvantages versus targets.

- **Digital Infrastructure Investment**: Modernize customer-facing and operational systems to enable personalization, self-service options, real-time disruption management, and data-driven decision making across the operation.

## 5. BOTTOM LINE

April 2026 reveals an airline successfully capturing demand but failing to deliver operational reliability and customer satisfaction, creating an unsustainable trajectory. Strong revenue growth and load factors mask deteriorating service quality that is eroding customer loyalty and inflating costs through operational inefficiency. Immediate focus must center on stabilizing operational performance—particularly on-time performance and cancellations—as these drive both customer experience and cost outcomes. Without rapid intervention on reliability and customer advocacy, the current gap between capacity success and execution quality will translate into competitive vulnerability and margin compression despite favorable market conditions.
