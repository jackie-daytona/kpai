## 3. TOP 3 CONCERNS

**1. On-Time Performance Deterioration (78.1%, -3.1pp YoY)**
On-time performance has fallen to critical levels, missing target by 6.9 percentage points and declining year-over-year despite typically favorable April weather conditions. This operational decline creates cascading negative effects including increased crew costs from duty-time violations, reduced aircraft utilization, higher passenger compensation expenses, and direct customer dissatisfaction. The correlation between OTP decline and falling CSAT/NPS scores suggests this operational weakness is the primary driver of customer experience deterioration. Root causes likely include ground handling inefficiencies, turnaround time challenges, maintenance scheduling issues, or air traffic control delays that require immediate diagnostic analysis.

**2. Customer Loyalty Collapse (NPS: 38, -6 YoY)**
Net Promoter Score has declined sharply to 38, falling 13.6% year-over-year and sitting 24% below target, signaling severe erosion in customer advocacy and loyalty. This metric predicts future revenue challenges as dissatisfied customers reduce rebooking rates, switch to competitors, and generate negative social media sentiment that damages brand reputation. The simultaneous decline in both NPS and CSAT, combined with deteriorating operational metrics, indicates customers are experiencing tangible service failures rather than minor perception issues. This trend threatens market share and pricing power in an increasingly competitive environment where customer retention is significantly more cost-effective than acquisition.

**3. Cost Inflation Outpacing Revenue Growth (CASM: 15.1¢, +5.6% YoY)**
Unit costs increased 5.6% year-over-year while revenue per ASM grew 7.0%, creating a narrowing margin gap that threatens profitability despite strong top-line performance. The 4.1% cost overrun versus target suggests either uncontrolled expense categories (fuel, labor, maintenance) or operational inefficiencies from delays and cancellations driving incremental crew costs and reduced asset productivity. With costs rising faster than historical trends while operational performance deteriorates, the airline faces margin compression that could accelerate if operational challenges persist. The gap between cost growth and operational performance improvement indicates expenses are rising from inefficiency rather than strategic investments in service quality.

## 4. PRIORITY RECOMMENDATIONS

### IMMEDIATE (0-30 Days)

- Launch cross-functional war room to diagnose on-time performance root causes through daily flight-level analysis of delay codes, focus on top 5 delay categories, and implement station-specific recovery protocols at underperforming hubs
- Implement enhanced customer recovery program including proactive rebooking for at-risk connections, expanded compensation authorization for frontline staff, and direct outreach to affected frequent flyers to prevent further NPS erosion
- Conduct emergency cost audit focusing on operational disruption expenses including unscheduled overtime, passenger compensation, and fuel inefficiencies from delays to identify immediate cost containment opportunities

### SHORT-TERM (30-90 Days)

- Deploy predictive analytics for maintenance and crew scheduling to reduce cancellations, optimizing buffer times and resource positioning based on historical disruption patterns
- Establish turnaround time improvement program at key stations through ground handling process reengineering, equipment upgrades, and cross-training initiatives to address OTP bottlenecks
- Launch customer experience task force to implement quick-win service enhancements in high-impact areas like boarding processes, communication during irregularities, and digital self-service tools
- Develop cost efficiency initiatives targeting the gap between actual and target CASM through fuel burn optimization, vendor contract renegotiation, and automation of manual processes

### STRATEGIC (90+ Days)

- Invest in integrated operations control technology including AI-powered disruption management, real-time resource optimization, and predictive delay prevention systems to sustainably improve operational reliability
- Redesign network schedule with adequate block times and aircraft/crew buffers based on actual operational performance data to build realistic, reliable schedules that support improved OTP
- Implement comprehensive voice-of-customer program with closed-loop feedback mechanisms, service recovery tracking, and employee incentive alignment to NPS improvement
- Develop multi-year cost transformation roadmap addressing structural cost drivers through fleet modernization analysis, maintenance strategy optimization, and strategic sourcing initiatives

## 5. BOTTOM LINE

April 2026 reveals an airline generating strong revenue growth and demand but suffering from operational execution failures that are destroying customer loyalty and eroding cost efficiency. The 3.1-point decline in on-time performance and 6-point NPS drop represent tangible deterioration in service delivery that threatens competitive positioning despite favorable market conditions. Immediate focus must shift to operational stabilization—particularly OTP improvement and cancellation reduction—while simultaneously controlling the cost inflation that threatens to consume revenue gains. Without rapid intervention to restore operational reliability, the current trajectory risks permanent customer defection and margin compression that will undermine long-term financial performance.
