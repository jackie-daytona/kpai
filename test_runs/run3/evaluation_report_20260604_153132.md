## 3. TOP 3 CONCERNS

**1. On-Time Performance Collapse Driving Customer Dissatisfaction**
The 3.1pp year-over-year decline in OTP to 78.1% represents the airline's most critical operational failure, falling 6.9pp short of target. This performance deterioration directly correlates with the 6-point NPS drop and 0.2-point CSAT decline, creating a cascading negative effect on customer loyalty. Poor OTP also drives incremental costs through crew overtime, passenger compensation, and irregular operations recovery, directly contributing to the 5.6% CASM increase. The combination of operational unreliability and rising customer dissatisfaction creates a vicious cycle threatening both near-term financial performance and long-term competitive positioning.

**2. Cost Growth Outpacing Revenue Growth Rate, Compressing Margins**
While revenue grew 8.4% year-over-year, CASM increased 5.6%, creating margin compression that undermines profitability despite strong demand. The $80M revenue shortfall against target combined with above-target costs represents a significant budget variance requiring immediate correction. Cost increases appear driven by operational inefficiencies (poor OTP and elevated cancellations requiring recovery resources) rather than strategic investments, making them particularly concerning. Without intervention, continued cost pressure will eliminate the competitive advantage gained from revenue improvements.

**3. Customer Loyalty Metrics in Freefall Despite Strong Load Factors**
The 13.6% NPS decline year-over-year to 38 indicates customers are flying out of necessity or lack of alternatives rather than preference, creating a vulnerable revenue base. This disconnect between high load factors (86.3%) and deteriorating loyalty metrics suggests the airline is winning on price or schedule but losing on execution and experience. Current demand strength masks underlying customer dissatisfaction that will materialize as churn once competitors improve offerings or economic conditions soften. The gap between operational performance and customer expectations is widening rather than closing.

## 4. PRIORITY RECOMMENDATIONS

### IMMEDIATE (0-30 days)

**On-Time Performance Recovery Task Force:** Establish daily operational review meetings to identify and resolve top delay causes, focusing on turn-time optimization, crew scheduling adjustments, and maintenance coordination.

**Cost Containment Blitz:** Implement immediate controls on discretionary spending and launch forensic analysis of CASM increase drivers, with particular focus on irregular operations costs and vendor contract compliance.

**Customer Service Recovery Protocols:** Deploy enhanced compensation and communication procedures for delays and cancellations, empowering frontline staff with authority to resolve issues immediately.

### SHORT-TERM (30-90 days)

**Operational Reliability Program:** Conduct comprehensive audit of aircraft maintenance scheduling, crew rostering practices, and ground handling procedures to identify systemic bottlenecks causing OTP and cancellation rate issues.

**Revenue Optimization Initiative:** Analyze the $80M revenue gap through route-level performance reviews, ancillary revenue attachment rates, and corporate contract compliance to identify quick-win recovery opportunities.

**Baggage Handling Excellence Expansion:** Replicate successful baggage performance improvements across all stations and extend best practices to other ground service touchpoints affecting customer experience.

### STRATEGIC (90+ days)

**Network and Fleet Optimization Review:** Evaluate whether current network structure, aircraft utilization patterns, and hub operations are sustainable given operational performance constraints, considering schedule adjustments to improve reliability.

**Customer Experience Transformation:** Design and implement end-to-end service delivery improvements addressing root causes of NPS decline, including technology investments in rebooking systems, proactive communication tools, and loyalty program enhancements.

**Operational Efficiency Modernization:** Invest in predictive maintenance capabilities, crew optimization algorithms, and integrated operations control systems to structurally improve OTP while reducing CASM through better asset utilization.

## 5. BOTTOM LINE

April 2026 reveals an airline growing revenue but struggling with operational execution and rapidly deteriorating customer loyalty. Immediate focus must address operational reliability as the root cause driving both cost overruns and customer dissatisfaction, while protecting revenue gains through enhanced service recovery.
