## 3. TOP 3 CONCERNS

**1. Operational Reliability Deterioration**
On-time performance has fallen 3.1pp YoY to 78.1%, while cancellations increased to 2.1%, creating a compounding negative impact on customer experience and operational costs. These metrics directly correlate with the 6-point NPS decline and CSAT reduction, indicating passengers are experiencing diminished service quality. Root causes likely include crew scheduling challenges, maintenance delays, or network complexity issues that require immediate diagnostic analysis and intervention.

**2. Cost Inflation Outpacing Revenue Growth**
Unit costs have risen 5.6% YoY to 15.1¢, now exceeding target by 4.1%, while revenue per ASM growth of 7.0% provides insufficient margin protection. This cost trajectory threatens profitability sustainability and competitive pricing flexibility. The gap suggests operational inefficiencies from irregular operations, fuel management issues, or uncontrolled overhead expenses requiring comprehensive cost structure review.

**3. Customer Advocacy Collapse**
NPS has declined from 44 to 38, representing a 13.6% deterioration that signals fundamental erosion in customer loyalty and brand strength. Combined with falling CSAT scores, this trend indicates operational failures are translating into lasting reputational damage. Recovery will require sustained operational improvement and proactive service recovery efforts to rebuild trust and competitive differentiation.

## 4. PRIORITY RECOMMENDATIONS

### IMMEDIATE (0-30 days)

- **Operational Recovery Task Force**: Establish daily cross-functional stand-ups with operations, maintenance, and crew scheduling to identify and resolve completion factor impediments affecting on-time performance and cancellations.
- **Customer Service Intervention**: Deploy enhanced service recovery protocols and proactive communication for disrupted passengers to mitigate further NPS erosion.
- **Cost Containment Audit**: Launch immediate review of discretionary spending and overtime utilization to address cost per ASM overruns.

### SHORT-TERM (30-90 days)

- **Schedule Optimization**: Conduct network complexity analysis to identify block time adjustments, turn time improvements, and hub connection vulnerabilities driving delays and cancellations.
- **Yield Management Enhancement**: Implement dynamic pricing refinements and ancillary revenue initiatives to close RASM gap to target while maintaining load factor momentum.
- **Reliability Incentive Program**: Introduce station-level performance accountability with transparent metrics tracking to drive operational discipline improvements.

### STRATEGIC (90+ days)

- **Operational Infrastructure Investment**: Develop comprehensive plan addressing fleet reliability, ground equipment modernization, and technology systems supporting predictable operations.
- **Cost Structure Transformation**: Design multi-year efficiency program targeting maintenance practices, fuel hedging strategy, and organizational productivity to achieve sustainable unit cost targets.
- **Customer Experience Redesign**: Implement end-to-end journey mapping with targeted investments in pain point elimination, service differentiation, and loyalty program enhancements to rebuild advocacy metrics.

## 5. BOTTOM LINE

April 2026 reveals an airline at an inflection point where strong demand fundamentals are being undermined by operational execution failures and cost discipline gaps. The 3.1pp decline in on-time performance and 6-point NPS deterioration demonstrate that operational reliability issues are directly damaging customer relationships and competitive positioning, while unit cost growth threatens margin sustainability despite healthy revenue growth. Immediate focus must prioritize operational stabilization through completion factor improvement and cost containment, followed by systematic investments in infrastructure and processes that restore service reliability and customer advocacy to competitive levels.
