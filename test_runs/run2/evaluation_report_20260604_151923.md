# AIRLINE OPERATIONS ANALYSIS – APRIL 2026

## 1. OVERALL PERFORMANCE SUMMARY

April 2026 demonstrates mixed performance with concerning operational deterioration despite revenue growth. While financial metrics show year-over-year improvement with revenue up 8.4%, operational excellence has declined across all core metrics, with on-time performance down 3.1 percentage points and cancellations up 0.2 points versus prior year. Customer satisfaction metrics reflect this operational stress, with NPS dropping 6 points YoY, creating a critical misalignment between revenue growth and service delivery that threatens long-term competitiveness.

## 3. TOP 3 CONCERNS

1. **On-Time Performance Crisis (78.1%, -3.1pp YoY)** – This represents the core operational failure driving all other negative metrics. Poor OTP creates customer dissatisfaction, increases costs through irregular operations, and reduces effective capacity. The 6.9-point gap to target translates to roughly 1 in 5 flights delayed, eroding customer trust and brand value while generating approximately $15-20M in estimated monthly recovery costs.

2. **NPS Freefall (38, -6 points YoY, -24% vs target)** – The 6-point decline in a single year signals customers are actively moving from promoters to detractors, threatening long-term revenue sustainability. At current booking values, this degradation could represent $50-75M in annual revenue risk from reduced repeat bookings and negative word-of-mouth, with recovery typically requiring 18-24 months.

3. **Rising Cost Structure Outpacing Revenue Growth (CASM +5.6% vs RASM +7.0%)** – While revenue is growing, the narrowing spread combined with 4.1% cost overrun indicates margin compression risk. Operational irregularities are driving premium costs (crew overtime, maintenance expedites, passenger compensation) that could eliminate profitability if operational metrics don't improve rapidly.
