# APRIL 2026 KPI PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS

## 1. OVERALL PERFORMANCE SUMMARY

April 2026 demonstrates mixed performance with revenue growth offset by operational and customer experience challenges. While revenue increased 8.4% YoY and load factor improved, on-time performance deteriorated significantly, and customer satisfaction metrics declined sharply. Cost pressures and operational inconsistencies are undermining the airline's competitive position despite strong demand fundamentals.

## 3. TOP 3 CONCERNS

**1. Operational Reliability Collapse**
On-time performance at 78.1% combined with 2.1% cancellation rate represents a fundamental breakdown in operational execution. These metrics are highly correlated with the customer satisfaction decline and create cascading cost impacts through crew overtime, aircraft repositioning, and passenger compensation. The worsening YoY trend indicates structural issues in crew scheduling, maintenance planning, or network design that require immediate intervention. This reliability crisis is the primary driver of both customer dissatisfaction and cost inflation.

**2. Customer Loyalty Deterioration**
NPS decline of 6 points to 38, combined with CSAT dropping to 4.1, signals severe customer experience degradation that threatens long-term competitiveness. These metrics lag operational performance, meaning current poor reliability will continue damaging scores even after improvements begin. Customer defection to competitors typically accelerates once NPS falls below 40, creating potential revenue spiral. The correlation between operational metrics and customer scores is clear and quantifiable.

**3. Cost Inflation Outpacing Revenue Growth**
CASM increased 5.6% YoY while RASM grew only 7.0%, with CASM exceeding target by 4.1% while RASM misses by 3.2%, demonstrating margin compression. The cost inflation appears driven by operational inefficiency rather than strategic investments, as evidenced by correlation with delay and cancellation rates. Without addressing root operational causes, this cost trajectory will eliminate profitability gains from strong demand environment.

## 4. PRIORITY RECOMMENDATIONS

### IMMEDIATE (0-30 days)

- **Operational Recovery Task Force**: Establish daily control center focused exclusively on reducing cancellations through enhanced maintenance forecasting, crew reserve optimization, and proactive aircraft swaps.
- **Customer Service Intervention**: Deploy executive-level service recovery team with enhanced compensation authority for affected passengers to stabilize NPS and prevent further CSAT erosion.
- **Cost Containment Blitz**: Implement immediate controls on non-essential spending and delay-related expenses including crew overtime restrictions and vendor renegotiations for ground handling.

### SHORT-TERM (30-90 days)

- **Schedule Rationalization**: Reduce block times by 5-7% on chronically delayed routes and eliminate marginal frequency on underperforming markets to improve completion factor and OTP.
- **Revenue Optimization Initiative**: Overhaul yield management algorithms to capture premium segment willingness-to-pay and implement dynamic ancillary bundling to close RASM gap.
- **Predictive Maintenance Acceleration**: Deploy data analytics to identify aircraft reliability patterns and shift to condition-based rather than calendar-based maintenance to reduce technical cancellations.
- **Frontline Empowerment Program**: Provide gate agents and flight attendants enhanced service recovery tools and authority to resolve customer issues immediately.

### STRATEGIC (90+ days)

- **Network Redesign Analysis**: Conduct comprehensive evaluation of hub connectivity patterns, aircraft utilization, and crew base positioning to eliminate structural operational vulnerabilities.
- **Fleet Commonality Assessment**: Evaluate long-term fleet strategy to reduce complexity-driven costs and improve maintenance efficiency and crew flexibility.
- **Customer Experience Transformation**: Implement end-to-end journey mapping with technology investments in rebooking automation, proactive notification systems, and self-service recovery options.
- **Operational Excellence Culture**: Establish performance-based incentive structures across all operational teams tied directly to OTP, completion factor, and customer metrics.

## 5. BOTTOM LINE

April 2026 performance reveals an airline experiencing demand strength but failing on operational execution, resulting in customer satisfaction collapse and margin compression. The 3.1 percentage point decline in on-time performance is directly driving the 6-point NPS drop and contributing significantly to the 5.6% cost inflation. Immediate focus must address operational reliability through schedule optimization and maintenance improvements, while simultaneously stabilizing customer perception through enhanced service recovery. Without rapid intervention on operational fundamentals, strong revenue momentum will reverse as customers defect and cost pressures eliminate profitability despite favorable market conditions.
