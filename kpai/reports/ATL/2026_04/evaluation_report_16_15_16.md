The airline is achieving volume growth but failing on core operational and customer experience metrics.

## 3. TOP 3 CONCERNS

**1. Operational Reliability Collapse**
On-time performance and cancellation rates have both deteriorated significantly, with OTP declining 3.1pp YoY to 78.1% and cancellations rising to 2.1%. This represents a fundamental breakdown in operational execution affecting roughly 1 in 4 flights. The simultaneous decline in both metrics suggests systemic issues in crew scheduling, maintenance, or ground operations rather than isolated problems. This reliability crisis is the primary driver of declining customer satisfaction scores.

**2. Customer Experience Deterioration**
Both CSAT and NPS are declining sharply (down 0.2 and 6 points YoY respectively), creating a dangerous negative feedback loop. A 14% NPS decline indicates customers are shifting from promoters to detractors, which will impact future booking behavior and brand perception. The correlation between operational failures and customer sentiment decline is clear and threatens long-term market position and pricing power.

**3. Cost Structure Erosion**
CASM has increased 5.6% year-over-year to 15.1¢, outpacing RASM growth of 7.0% but creating compression risk. Costs are exceeding target by 4.1% while sitting 0.6¢ above planned levels, suggesting either operational inefficiency or unplanned expense escalation. Without intervention, the margin between revenue and cost growth will narrow further, threatening the sustainability of current operations and limiting strategic flexibility.

## 4. PRIORITY RECOMMENDATIONS

### IMMEDIATE (0-30 days)
- Establish daily operational war room to identify and resolve root causes of delays and cancellations through cross-functional teams
- Implement customer recovery protocols for affected passengers, including proactive communication and compensation frameworks to stop NPS bleeding
- Conduct emergency audit of crew scheduling and maintenance workflows to identify immediate bottlenecks causing reliability issues

### SHORT-TERM (30-90 days)
- Deploy predictive analytics for delay prevention, focusing on turnaround time optimization and preemptive crew/aircraft repositioning
- Launch targeted ancillary revenue initiatives and dynamic pricing refinements to close the RASM gap to target
- Implement cost containment task force to identify quick-win efficiency improvements in top expense categories driving CASM increases

### STRATEGIC (90+ days)
- Redesign operational processes and invest in technology infrastructure to systematically improve completion factor and on-time performance
- Develop comprehensive customer experience transformation program linking operational reliability to satisfaction recovery
- Execute structural cost reduction program addressing labor productivity, fuel efficiency, and vendor contract optimization to restore competitive cost position

## 5. BOTTOM LINE

Q4 2026 reveals an airline generating revenue growth but failing on operational fundamentals that drive customer loyalty and sustainable profitability. The simultaneous deterioration of on-time performance, customer satisfaction, and cost efficiency creates a critical situation requiring immediate intervention. Without rapid operational stabilization and cost discipline, revenue gains will prove temporary as customers defect and margins compress. Leadership must prioritize reliability recovery as the foundation for financial performance improvement.
